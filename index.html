About

I am a Master's student in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Calgary, supervised by Professor Philipp Woelfel. My work focuses on distributed algorithms in shared-memory systems and the theory of distributed computing. My research interests include discrete and distributed algorithms, distributed systems and theoretical computer science with emerging interests in database systems, blockchain, and bioinformatics. Before starting my MSc, I earned a Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering from Sharif University of Technology in 2023. Outside of my research, I'm always happy to connect about computer science, algorithms, or anything else; the best way to contact me is through my university email.

Research / Learning

My current agenda focuses on building practical wait-free synchronization with strong progress guarantees.

  • Wait-free multi-locking for multi-object coordination.
  • Randomized algorithms under weak/adaptive adversaries.
  • Linearizability, compositional proofs, and testing harnesses.
