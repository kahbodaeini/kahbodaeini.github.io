About

I am a Master's student in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Calgary, supervised by Professor Philipp Woelfel. My work focuses on distributed algorithms in shared-memory systems and the theory of distributed computing. My research interests include discrete and distributed algorithms, distributed systems, and theoretical computer science, with emerging interests in database systems, blockchain, and bioinformatics. Before starting my MSc, I earned a Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering from Sharif University of Technology in 2023. I also have experience as a head teaching assistant and teaching assistant for both undergraduate and graduate courses in UCalgary and SUT. Outside of my research, I'm always happy to connect about computer science, algorithms, or anything else; the best way to contact me is through my university email.

Research / Learning

My research focuses on designing efficient, provably correct distributed algorithms in shared-memory systems, with particular attention to wait-free and lock-free synchronization. I am especially interested in how theoretical models, together with my knowledge of randomization and probability theory, can inform the design of concurrent systems. My goal is to bridge the gap between theory and practice by developing algorithms that are not only efficient but also straightforward to implement and use in real-world applications.
Alongside my research, I actively broaden my expertise in areas that complement and extend my work in distributed computing and algorithms. I am particularly interested in algorithms of all kinds, blockchain systems, distributed databases, database theory, complexity theory, and theoretical computer science more generally. I also maintain an active interest in bioinformatics, exploring its algorithmic and data-driven challenges.
